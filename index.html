Venue spotlight

Fujiya Hotel & Kikka-so

Fujiya Hotel opened in 1891 and has been hosting heads of state, artists, and travelers ever since. 120 rooms across the main building, enough to put your entire group on site. The main dining room (pictured) handles your reception in the standard package: coffered painted ceilings, deep green walls, herringbone parquet, an unmistakable Meiji-era Western-Japanese fusion. On the same grounds sits Kikka-so, a separate Meiji-era imperial villa available as a ceremony upgrade for couples who want something more intimate and traditionally Japanese. Fujiya quoted us a wedding package at ¥65,000 per person that includes venue, ceremony, the full reception meal, an open bar, and one night's accommodation per guest. That last piece is the unlock: nobody books a hotel separately, nobody coordinates check-in, the entire group sleeps where the wedding happens. The hotel also confirmed directly that the reception can include music and dancing. A real party, not a polite one.

The Hidden Garden.

Venue spotlight

Togo Kinenkan

A heritage building behind Togo Shrine in Harajuku, with a private courtyard garden you'd never guess was there from the street. Intimate, hidden, tucked in plain sight. The terrace handles your ceremony. Tamayura, the reception hall, has floor-to-ceiling yakusugi cedar windows that look out into the garden. We've placed multiple international couples here. The venue hosts more than 100 international weddings a year and knows exactly how to handle guests flying in from two continents.
